# Behavioral Science for Search Ads (RSAs)

Strategies for Search Ads to ensure they are simple, persuasive, and action-oriented.

## Simplicity and Clarity

- **Keep Ads Simple**: Use few elements, large fonts, and simple language
- **Efficiency**: Reduce the word count to save space for value propositions (e.g., social proof)
- **User-Centric Language**: Focus on what the user gets rather than the brand's perspective

## Persuasion Principles (Applicable to RSA Headlines/Descriptions)

- **Social Proof**: Directly state the popularity or reliability of the offer
- **Urgency/Scarcity**: Use time-based or supply-based scarcity (e.g., "Only X left," "Offer ends soon") to prompt immediate action
- **Anticipation**: Use copy that visualizes the future reward of the product/service
- **Reason Why + Loss Aversion**: Use "because" to provide a reason for the action and frame the copy around what the user stands to lose if they do not act (e.g., missing out on a benefit)
- **Direct Answer**: Ensure the ad directly addresses the question the user asked in their search query

## CTA Best Practices

- Make your Call to Action (CTA) clear, actionable, and aligned with the user's intent
- Experiment with different CTA formulations to see what yields the highest conversion rates

## Broader Principles for Copywriting

- **Curiosity**: Use headlines that create an "information gap," such as "This is how you can..." or "See what... looks like on the inside"
- **Exceptional Benefit**: Reframe offers around the strongest perceived benefit or transformation
- **Simple Questions**: Where appropriate, turn static CTAs into simple, human-friendly questions
- **Unfinished Journey**: Use copy that implies an ongoing or incomplete process (e.g., "One last step...") to drive completion
